Revitalizing Ecosystems in Greater Boston to Survive Climate Change Conference - Register Now

Ecosystems across our highly developed region are threatened by climate change. But these ecosystems are also our best allies. Local ecosystems can help us to weather the coming climate shocks, and there is much that we can do to revitalize them in our yards, streets, neighborhoods, parks, wetlands and waters. Come to this conference to be inspired. Learn about current efforts and new possibilities for revitalizing ecosystems.

As Giant Storms Hammer Boston, Officials are Doing Little to Prepare for Them (Washington Post 3/4/18)

“Boston will return to its historic boundary lines,” said Emily Norton, director of the Massachusetts chapter of the Sierra Club and a city councilor in Newton, west of Boston. Talk and theories tossed around at a commission meeting won’t stop it, she said.

“You see the talk, but you don’t see the action,” said Norton. “They’re talking about spending billions of dollars for a sea wall and dikes, hardening the shore. Boston should be doing more. Massachusetts is a state that others follow, and Massachusetts has to do more. We have to take steps to reverse this and get off of fossil fuels.”
