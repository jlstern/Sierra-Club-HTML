The Boston City Council will be voting on a plastic bag ban this coming Wednesday, Nov. 29th.

Boston residents are estimated to use over 200 million polyethylene shopping bags per year. There is no excuse for single-use shopping bags made of petrochemicals derived from non-renewable, fracked natural gas and oil.

Boston is the largest municipality in New England. Fifty-nine cities and towns in Massachusetts have already banned plastic bags. Let's make Boston the 60th!

Here are some of the reasons why we should ban plastic bags...

  • Bags are made from fossil fuels so contribute to our dependence on petroleum.
  • They are a major litter problem, thanks especially to their aerodynamic qualities.
  • Plastic bags can break recycling equipment, costing the city money and time to repair.
  • They are dangerous to both domestic and wild animals, who eat them and die.
  • As part of the ordinance outreach, free reusable bags will be distributed in low-income areas and to seniors. As Councilor Pressley points out, low-income communities suffer the most from trash due to single-use bags and shouldnâ€™t be used as an excuse not to ban plastic bags.
  • Plastic bags didn't exist until the 1970s. We all managed just fine without them.
  • Furthermore, recycling cannot solve the waste problem, because it is inconvenient and has resulted in return rates of only 5%.
