Last week we saw the impacts of climate change on our City. Thursday’s storm brought the highest tide ever recorded - higher than the Blizzard of ‘78 - and such extreme flooding that a resident captured video of a dumpster floating down the street. Unless we act now, Boston faces much more catastrophic storms in the future.

Please help us make Boston a climate leader, not a climate laggard!

Help us oppose new gas pipelines!

National Grid plans to construct a one-mile fracked gas pipeline in the Back Bay/South End to service new high-rise condos and luxury hotels. We should be transitioning OFF fossil fuels, not building more. More fracked gas means Boston will have no hope of reaching Mayor Walsh’s goal of carbon neutrality by 2050.

Please attend a public meeting to say NO to this new pipeline!
When: January 16th, 6:00 to 7:30 pm
Where: Boston Central Public Library, 700 Boylston Street, Rabb Lecture Hall

RSVP here to attend the public meeting, and help us spread the word by inviting your friends and neighbors!

The Boston Climate Action Network (BCAN) is hosting a party to celebrate this victory with fellow green justice allies and climate activists. All are welcome! There will be food and beverages, and we'll talk about what's next to get the City to follow through on its implementation of CCE.
